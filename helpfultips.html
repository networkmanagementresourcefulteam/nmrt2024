TIPS OF THE DAY NPS/UES

 Scenarios:

 OPH linkage to UHC

♦ If provider/group has contract with Optum, HCP must contact Optum for any information.
♦ If HCP insist on getting contracted through UHC due to Medical procedure, provider must create a new TIN and request cred/contracting process with that new TIN


 Credentialing Denial

♦ If denial is over 30 days, we can provide the reason of denial and advise them to submit initial credentialing
♦ If denial is within 30 days, look for the reason of denial, advise why is being denied and to submit the need documentation, if any
 Filling Contracting Documents

♦ If HCP is trying to update anything on the contract, this will need to be transferred to the Network Account Manager (NAM)
♦ If they are requesting help on filling out blanks of a form, we can request the screenshot of the form and advise brief description of what they need to input on the blanks (please), do not provide PHI for the HCP to fill the blanks


 Market Strategy Grid (MSG)

♦ If provider is credentialed and they are looking to add a product, we should review the MSG to verify if the market for those specific products are open (above/blank), closed with possibility of exception (below) or closed without   exception (N/A), based of their specialty and    market number
 Contract/HPDemo

♦ If the HCP is requesting contracts for the first time, meaning they have never been contracted with UHC (no C2/P3 contracts on IEZ screen), we can refer them to initiate request through HPDemo
♦ If contract request is for a new TIN, HCP can be referred to HPDemo, in the case the HCP insist or is upset, we can go the extra mile and submit the PhyCon for them
♦ If HCP is contracted with UHC (with contracts C2/P3) we have to initiate the contracting request
 Credentialing in progress

♦ If on IDC screen you see status under code 170 for more the 30 days, this should be referred to PSV Restart Team
♦ If on IDC screen you see status under code 170 within 30 days and we are missing documentation, let the HCP know which documentation we are missing for process to be completed
♦ If current cycle has a future due date, however status is code 800s/900s this mean the credentialing has been denied and provider needs initial credentialing or to provide documentation to have credentialing back up without   occurring to interruptions on their contracts

 How do I submit my disclosure form?


You can submit the Provider Disclosure of Ownership and Control Interest Statement form in several ways:

• Online: Go to UHCprovider.com> Resources > Health Plans > Choose a Location > UnitedHealthcare Community Plan Homepage > Provider Forms and References > Disclosure of Ownership and Control Interest Form
• Secure email: Please email uhc_disclosures@uhc.com and attach your completed disclosure form
• Secure fax: 866-562-7184
• Mail:
         UnitedHealthcare Community Plan
         Attn: C&S Forms Compliance
         2300 W. Plano Pkwy.
         #C1E105
         Plano, TX 75075

  I have already submitted a disclosure. Why do I have to resubmit it?
 The Provider Disclosure of Ownership and Control Interest Statement form must be submitted with your initial Participation Agreement. The form must also be resubmitted:
• Every 3 years thereafter
• Any time there is a revision to the information. The updated form must be provided within 35 days of the change.
• When updated information is requested. The statement must be provided within 35 days of a request for this information.

 What is Knowledge-Based Authentication (KBA)?


This is a high-level authentication used by Adobe Sign to validate the signer’s authenticity to protect the privacy and security of their information. The signer is asked a number of customized questions related to their current public information, based on their Social Security number. They must get all the answers correct or they will not be able to sign the agreement
